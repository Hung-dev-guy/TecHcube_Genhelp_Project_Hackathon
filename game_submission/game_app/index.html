📚 Sexual Health Education

🛡️ Safe Sex & Protection

Condoms: The most effective way to prevent STIs and pregnancy. Use them correctly every time.

Birth Control: Various methods including pills, IUDs, implants. Consult healthcare providers.

Emergency Contraception: Available within 72 hours after unprotected sex.

🧬 STI Prevention & Awareness

Common STIs: Chlamydia, gonorrhea, syphilis, HPV, herpes, HIV/AIDS.

Testing: Get tested regularly if sexually active. Many STIs have no symptoms.

Treatment: Most STIs are treatable. Early detection is crucial.

Prevention: Use protection, limit partners, get vaccinated (HPV, Hepatitis B).

💬 Consent & Communication

What is Consent? Freely given, reversible, informed, enthusiastic, and specific (FRIES).

Communication: Talk openly with partners about boundaries, desires, and safety.

Saying No: You have the right to say no at any time. Respect others' boundaries.

Healthy Relationships: Built on trust, respect, communication, and equality.

🧠 Mental Health & Wellbeing

Body Image: Everyone's body is different. Love and respect yourself.

Peer Pressure: It's okay to wait. Make decisions that feel right for you.

Support: Talk to trusted adults, counselors, or helplines if you need help.

Resources: Planned Parenthood, local health clinics, school counselors.

🌟 Your Rights

Bodily Autonomy: Your body belongs to you. You decide what happens to it.

Privacy: Healthcare providers keep your information confidential.

Access to Care: You have the right to sexual health services.

Education: You deserve accurate, comprehensive sex education.
